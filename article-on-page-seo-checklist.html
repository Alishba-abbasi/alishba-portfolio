On-Page SEO Checklist for Every Page

What Is On-Page SEO?

On-page SEO refers to all the optimizations you make directly on your website's pages to help them rank higher in search results. Unlike off-page or technical SEO, on-page SEO is entirely within your control — it is about how you structure and write your content.

If you have not yet read our foundational guide, start with What Is SEO? A Beginner's Complete Guide before diving into this checklist. And if you have not done your keyword research yet, check out the Keyword Research Guide for Beginners first — on-page SEO only works once you know which keywords to target.

Title Tags

Your title tag is the clickable headline shown in search results. It is one of the strongest on-page ranking signals and also the first thing users see when deciding whether to click your link.

✓Keep your title under 60 characters so it doesn't get cut off in search results.
✓Place your primary keyword near the beginning of the title.
✓Make it compelling — it should make someone want to click, not just describe the page.
✓Avoid keyword stuffing — one natural mention is enough.

Meta Descriptions

The meta description is the short summary shown below your title in search results. While it does not directly impact rankings, a well-written meta description significantly improves your click-through rate.

✓Keep it between 150–160 characters.
✓Include your target keyword naturally.
✓Write it like an ad — clearly state the value the reader will get.

Heading Structure

Headings (H1, H2, H3) organize your content for both readers and search engines. A clear hierarchy helps Google understand what your page is about and helps readers scan your content quickly.

  • Use exactly one H1 per page — this should contain your main keyword and match the page's core topic.
  • Use H2s for your main sections and H3s for subsections within those.
  • Never skip heading levels (don't jump from H2 straight to H4).
  • Make headings descriptive — a reader should understand the page just by scanning the headings.

Keyword Placement

Once you know your target keyword, place it strategically throughout your content — without overdoing it. Google's algorithms are smart enough to understand context and related terms, so natural writing always wins over forced repetition.

📍 Where to Place Keywords

Title tag, H1, the first 100 words of your content, at least one H2, naturally throughout the body, and in your image alt text.

Internal Linking

Internal links connect your pages to each other, helping both users and search engines navigate your site and understand how your content relates. They also help distribute "link equity" across your pages, boosting the authority of less-visible content.

Notice how this article links back to the Keyword Research Guide and forward to Technical SEO Basics — that is internal linking in action.

Images & Alt Text

Every image on your page should have descriptive alt text. This helps visually impaired users understand your content through screen readers, and it gives Google additional context about your page's topic — which can also help your images rank in Google Image search.

URL Structure

Clean, descriptive URLs are easier for both users and search engines to understand. A URL like /on-page-seo-checklist is far better than /page?id=482.

  • Keep URLs short and descriptive.
  • Use hyphens to separate words, not underscores.
  • Include your target keyword where natural.
  • Avoid unnecessary parameters and numbers.

Full On-Page SEO Checklist

✓Title tag under 60 characters with keyword near the front
✓Meta description between 150–160 characters
✓One clear H1 matching the page topic
✓Logical H2/H3 structure
✓Keyword in the first 100 words
✓At least 2–3 internal links to related content
✓Descriptive alt text on all images
✓Clean, short, keyword-friendly URL
✓Mobile-friendly formatting with short paragraphs

Conclusion

On-page SEO is the most controllable part of your SEO strategy because everything happens directly on your own pages. Run through this checklist every time you publish new content, and you will already be ahead of most beginners who skip these fundamentals.

Once your on-page elements are solid, the next layer to focus on is your site's technical foundation. Continue to Technical SEO Basics Explained.
