What Is Technical SEO?

Technical SEO refers to the behind-the-scenes optimizations that help search engines crawl, index, and understand your website properly. It is not about your content's writing quality — it is about whether Google can even access and process your content in the first place.

Even the best content in the world will not rank if technical issues are blocking Google from finding or indexing it. If you have not yet covered the basics, start with What Is SEO? A Beginner's Complete Guide for context on how crawling and indexing work.

Crawlability

Crawlability refers to how easily Google's bots can navigate through your website. If important pages are buried deep within your site structure or blocked by broken links, crawlers may never reach them.

  • Keep your site structure shallow — important pages should be reachable within 3 clicks from the homepage.
  • Fix broken internal links that lead crawlers to dead ends.
  • Use a logical internal linking strategy, similar to how this article links to On-Page SEO Checklist and other related guides.

Indexability

Even if Google can crawl a page, that doesn't guarantee it gets indexed (added to Google's database). Pages can be excluded from the index due to "noindex" tags, duplicate content issues, or low-quality signals.

🔍 Quick Check

Use Google Search Console's URL Inspection tool to instantly check whether a specific page is indexed and request indexing if it's missing. This is one of the free tools covered in our Best Free SEO Tools guide.

Site Speed

Page speed is both a ranking factor and a major driver of user experience. Slow-loading pages frustrate visitors and increase bounce rate, which signals to Google that your page may not be a good result.

  • Compress and resize images before uploading them.
  • Minimize unnecessary JavaScript and CSS files.
  • Use browser caching to speed up repeat visits.
  • Choose reliable, fast hosting.

Core Web Vitals

Core Web Vitals are a specific set of metrics Google uses to measure real-world user experience on your page.

✓Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) — measures loading performance. Should occur within 2.5 seconds.
✓Interaction to Next Paint (INP) — measures responsiveness to user interaction. Should be under 200ms.
✓Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS) — measures visual stability. Should be under 0.1.

Mobile-Friendliness

Google uses mobile-first indexing, meaning it primarily evaluates the mobile version of your website for ranking — even for desktop searches. A site that isn't fully responsive will struggle to rank regardless of how good the content is.

  • Use responsive design that adapts to all screen sizes.
  • Make sure buttons and links are large enough to tap easily.
  • Avoid intrusive pop-ups that block content on mobile.

Sitemaps & Robots.txt

An XML sitemap is a file that lists all the important pages on your website, helping Google discover and crawl them efficiently. A robots.txt file tells search engines which pages or sections they should or should not crawl.

⚙️ Setup Tip

Submit your XML sitemap directly through Google Search Console to speed up discovery of new pages — especially useful for newly launched websites.

HTTPS Security

HTTPS encrypts the connection between your website and visitors, protecting user data. It has been a confirmed Google ranking signal since 2014, and browsers now actively warn users when visiting non-HTTPS sites — which damages trust and increases bounce rate.

Conclusion

Technical SEO might sound intimidating, but it comes down to one core idea: make sure search engines can easily access, understand, and trust your website. Start by checking your site in Google Search Console, fixing any indexing issues, and improving your page speed and mobile experience.
